Fisheries Ecology and Conservation at MSU

Cline Lab — Department of Ecology, Montana State University

We develop a mechanistic understanding of what makes fish populations and fisheries productive and resilient — and apply that knowledge toward science-based conservation strategies. Our work centers on how streamflow variability and drought shape fish production, how life-history diversity buffers populations against environmental change, and how threatened species like bull trout can recover in a rapidly changing world. We work closely with state, federal, and tribal partners to ensure our science informs real management decisions.
