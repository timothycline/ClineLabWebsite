Bull trout in the Kootenai River basin face a combination of threats including altered hydrology from Libby Dam, non-native lake trout competition, and habitat fragmentation. This project builds on a prior USGS-funded study of Lake Koocanusa that quantified demographic drivers of productivity and recreational fishery potential (Cochrane et al., *Ecological Applications*, in press).

The current phase, led by postdoctoral researcher **Madaline Cochrane**, focuses on downstream populations below Libby Dam. Using an integrated population model that combines long-term monitoring data with new field collections, we are assessing how dam operations have influenced demographic connectivity, survival rates, and long-term population viability. This work is being conducted in close collaboration with Montana Fish, Wildlife & Parks and is intended to inform decisions about dam management, flow releases, and fisheries recovery targets.

**Key outputs:**
- Integrated population model for Kootenai River bull trout below Libby Dam
- Assessment of dam operation effects on demographic connectivity
- Management-relevant benchmarks for population recovery
