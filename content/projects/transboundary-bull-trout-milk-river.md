The Milk River straddles the U.S.–Canada border in north-central Montana and is subject to an international water compact that governs flow allocation between the two countries. Bull trout populations in this system depend on adequate instream flows during critical life stages, but the implications of current and future flow regimes for population persistence have not been rigorously assessed.

This project developed population models and flow-response relationships for Milk River bull trout, providing the scientific basis for instream flow targets that can be incorporated into compact negotiations and water management decisions. Work was conducted in collaboration with state and federal stakeholders on both sides of the border, with a focus on ensuring that population sustainability under a changing climate was explicitly accounted for in flow planning.

**Key outputs:**
- Population assessment for Milk River bull trout
- Flow-response models linking discharge to demographic rates
- Instream flow recommendations to support international compact obligations
