Timothy Cline is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Ecology at Montana State University. His research focuses on the ecology and management of cold-water fishes — particularly salmonids — in the context of climate change, habitat alteration, and recreational fisheries.

Before joining MSU, he held positions at US Geological Survey and University of Michigan and completed his PhD at University of Washington. He is broadly interested in population dynamics, life-history evolution, and using quantitative tools to bridge basic ecology and applied fisheries management.
