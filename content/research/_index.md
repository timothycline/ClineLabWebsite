## Overview

The Cline Lab studies the ecology and management of cold-water fishes — primarily salmonids — in western North America. We combine long-term field datasets, physiological experiments, and quantitative modeling to understand how environmental change shapes fish populations and to translate that understanding into actionable management guidance.

---

## Research Themes

### Climate Change & Thermal Physiology
We measure how rising water temperatures affect fish growth, survival, and reproduction, and use those data to forecast population-level vulnerability under future climate scenarios.

### Population Dynamics & Life History
Using mark-recapture and telemetry studies, we examine how environmental variability drives year-to-year fluctuations in population size, age structure, and life-history expression.

### Landscape Connectivity
We apply landscape genetics and movement ecology tools to identify which habitat features — dams, road crossings, stream temperature gradients — limit fish movement and gene flow among fragmented populations.

### Applied Fisheries Management
Our lab works closely with state and federal agencies to translate ecological findings into harvest regulations, habitat restoration priorities, and recovery plans.
