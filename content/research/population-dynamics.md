---
title: "Population Dynamics & Life History"
description: "Understanding how environmental variability drives fluctuations in population size, age structure, and life-history expression in salmonids."
weight: 2
---

Fish populations are not static — they pulse and shift in response to temperature, flow, food availability, and a host of interacting stressors. Understanding the mechanisms behind this variability is fundamental to predicting how populations will respond to management actions and to anticipating the consequences of climate change and habitat alteration.

We use long-term mark-recapture datasets, otolith microchemistry, and telemetry to track individual fish through time and reconstruct population-level dynamics. A recurring theme in our work is the role of **portfolio effects** — the way diversity in life-history strategies buffers populations against environmental extremes, much like a diversified financial portfolio reduces investment risk.

**Current directions include:**

- Long-term growth and survival monitoring in snowmelt-dominated Rocky Mountain streams
- Quantifying how variation in juvenile rearing habitat translates to adult population resilience
- Linking individual-level life-history variation (age at maturation, migration timing) to population stability
- Using state-space models to disentangle process from observation error in abundance time series
