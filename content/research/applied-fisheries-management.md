Ecological research only fulfills its potential when it connects to the decisions that shape landscapes and fisheries. A core commitment of the Cline Lab is co-producing knowledge with the managers and stakeholders who will use it — designing studies around management questions, sharing data and tools in accessible formats, and engaging in the messy but important process of translating science into policy.

We work closely with Montana Fish, Wildlife & Parks, the U.S. Forest Service, the Bureau of Land Management, and several Tribal fish and wildlife programs. Projects range from short-term technical assistance to long-term monitoring partnerships.

**Current directions include:**

- Providing thermal vulnerability assessments to support bull trout critical habitat designations
- Advising harvest regulations for recreational trout fisheries using escapement-based reference points
- Developing decision-support tools for habitat restoration prioritization under limited budgets
- Evaluating the effectiveness of voluntary flow agreements in sustaining fish populations during drought
